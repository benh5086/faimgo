# Faimgo Assessment — Design v2

**What changed from v1:** The assessment now starts with what the person *wants* (their direction), not their circumstances. Desire gets validated against motivation, experience, money, and time — the reality-check engine. The results never crush a dream; they sequence it: an honest verdict on the path they want, paired with a fast first win that funds it.

**Flow:** 3 sections × 4 questions (10–12 questions depending on branch, under 3 minutes) → insight break after each section → email gate → results.

## Section 1 — Your Direction (want → why → experience → time)

**Q1. Do you already have something in mind?**
- Yes — a specific thing I want to do → *(picker: the 9 paths in plain words + "Something else" with a short free-text box)*
- Sort of — a general area *(working with people / making things / online business / hands-on work)*
- No — that's why I'm here, match me

*The "Something else" free-text is captured with the lead — it's Faimgo market research: what people actually want that the library doesn't cover yet.*

**Q2. What's pulling you toward it?** *(only if Q1 = yes / sort of — the honesty question)*
- I've done it before, or something close to it
- It's something I love or already do for fun
- It looks like good money
- I've seen others succeed at it and want in

**Q3. How much do you actually know about it?** *(only if Q1 = yes / sort of)*
- I've done it professionally or seriously
- Hobby level — informal but real
- I've researched it but never done it
- Almost nothing yet, just interested

**Q4. How many hours a week can you realistically give this — working on it, learning it, all of it?**
- Less than 5 — squeezing it in
- 5–10 — a few evenings
- 10–20 — serious side commitment
- 20+ — this is a priority

**🔍 Insight break 1 — "Your starting profile"**
- Named-path users get a **reality meter** on their chosen path: **Pro start** (experience + real motivation), **Warm start** (love/hobby, some knowledge), or **Cold start** (money-drawn, no experience — phrased kindly: "You're starting from zero on this one. That's allowed — but the plan has to respect it.").
- Match-me users get the v1 speed profile (Sprinter / Builder / Hybrid) + "X of 9 paths in play" counter.

## Section 2 — Your Resources & Reality (money → tools → timeline → risk)

**Q5. How much could you put in to start — without stressing about it?**
- $0 — needs to be free to start
- Under $100
- $100–$500
- $500+ — I can invest in this

**Q6. What do you already have that could help you start? (select all that apply)**
- A reliable car
- A computer + solid internet
- Tools or equipment (lawn, cleaning, craft, camera…)
- Spare space (garage, storage, a spare room)
- Skills-related gear for the thing I named in Q1
- None of these right now

*Hard filters: no car → gig apps excluded; no computer → freelancing/VA/digital/content demoted; no space → reselling demoted.*
*Gap logic: if their (named or matched) path needs tools they don't have, the results plan includes a **Start Cheap Kit** — where to get low-cost starting tools and services: used/refurb marketplaces, renting vs. buying for the first jobs, free software tiers, borrowing, and what NOT to buy until the first dollar arrives. The assessment collects the gap; the plan fills it.*

**Q7. How soon do you want to see your first dollar from this?**
- This week if possible
- Within a month
- 1–3 months is fine
- No rush — I'm building something bigger

*Reality-check engine (the heart of v2): Q7 is checked against Q2/Q3 and the path's real speed. If someone picks a slow-ramp path (content, digital products, anything expert-level), has no experience, and wants money this week — the results say it straight: "Possible? Technically. Realistic? No — here's the honest ramp for [path], and here's what CAN pay you this week while you build it." Never mocking, never crushing — but never pretending.*

**Q8. Which sounds more like you?**
- Give me guaranteed small money over a risky big win
- Mostly sure things, with a little upside
- Balanced — some safe, some bets
- I'll bet my time on a bigger payoff

**🔍 Insight break 2 — "Your launch window"**
Names their asset + budget combo in plain words and what it unlocks (e.g. "A car, tools, and $100 to work with — you could take a first local-services job this weekend."). If a reality gap was detected, this is where the honest flag first appears — before the results, so the verdict doesn't blindside them. Counter updates: "Down to X paths."

## Section 3 — How You Work (fit & follow-through)

**Q9. What gives you energy?**
- Being around people, face to face
- People in small doses — online is ideal
- Mostly working alone
- Strictly solo, headphones on

**Q10. How do you feel about putting yourself out there online?**
- Love it — camera doesn't scare me
- Happy to post, prefer not on camera
- Behind the scenes only
- I'd rather work offline entirely

*Hard filter: offline-only → content creation excluded, online-facing paths demoted.*

**Q11. Which feels most like you?**
- Making and creating things
- Selling, negotiating, closing
- Helping and taking care of people
- Building systems and keeping things running

**Q12. What's gotten in the way before?**
- Never knew where to start
- Started things but lost steam
- Time — life keeps eating it
- Scared of wasting money
- This is my first real attempt

*(Q12 doesn't affect scoring — it sets the tone of their plan: "lost steam" → day-by-day walkthrough emphasis; "scared of wasting money" → lead with the $0-to-start step.)*

## Email gate (unchanged)

After Q12: **"Your two paths are ready."** One email field + **"Show me my plan."**
Microcopy: "We'll also send your plan so you don't lose it. No spam — you can unsubscribe anytime."
Results render client-side immediately after submit.

## Results page (v2 logic)

**If they named a path (Q1 = yes / sort of):**

**🎯 Your Chosen Path — [Path], reality-checked**
- An honest verdict with one of three framings:
  - **Green — "You can start this now."** Experience + resources line up. First 3 moves, first dollar estimate.
  - **Yellow — "You can get there — here's the real ramp."** Motivation or resources are partial. First 3 moves start with the cheapest possible validation step; Start Cheap Kit included if tools are missing.
  - **Red-but-kind — "Here's the truth about this path for you today."** Money-only motivation + no experience + unrealistic timeline. Shows the honest timeline and cost, then: "If you still want it after reading that, here's step one — and here's what pays you in the meantime."
- If Q1 was "Something else" (free text): a short honest note that their idea is outside the current library + the closest matching path + the general validation steps that apply to any idea.

**⚡ Your Fastest First Win — [Path]** *(always shown)*
- The highest-scoring Speed ≥ 4 path from their answers. Framed as the funder: "This pays while you build."
- Why it fits (2–3 sentences from their actual answers), first 3 moves, first-dollar estimate vs. the 30/60/90 promise.

**"How the two work together"** — one line, e.g. "Babysit weekends now; it funds the certification that makes it a real business by day 90."

**If they said "match me":** v1 logic unchanged — Fastest First Win + Best Long-Term Path, two cards, same one-line bridge.

**CTA:** subscribe to get walked through it step-by-step (the Faimgo core offer).

## Scoring mechanics (v2)

- Named path gets a **desire boost** (+4) but must pass hard filters and the reality-check engine; motivation weighting: experience (+3) > love/hobby (+2) > seen-others-succeed (+1) > money-only (+0).
- All other options carry v1-style weights (+0 to +3) toward relevant paths.
- Reality flag = slow path (Speed ≤ 2) AND no experience (Q3 bottom two) AND fast timeline (Q7 top choice). Flag switches the chosen-path card to Yellow/Red framing.
- Fastest First Win = top score among Speed ≥ 4 paths after filters.
- Long-Term (match-me flow) = top of (score × ceiling weight).
- All client-side, instant; free-text and answers stored with the email lead.
